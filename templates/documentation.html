Prediction Guide

A complete reference for understanding the medical terms and values used in our prediction models.

Heart Disease Input Parameters

  • Age: Age of the patient in years.
  • Sex: 1 = Male, 0 = Female.

Chest pain type (4 values):

  • 0: Typical angina (chest pain related to heart).
  • 1: Atypical angina (chest pain not related to heart).
  • 2: Non-anginal pain (non-heart related pain).
  • 3: Asymptomatic (no pain).

Resting blood pressure (in mm Hg on admission to the hospital).

Normal range is usually around 120/80.

Serum cholestoral in mg/dl.

Healthy is generally less than 200 mg/dL.

Fasting blood sugar > 120 mg/dl.

  • 1: True (High blood sugar)
  • 0: False (Normal blood sugar)

Resting electrocardiographic results:

  • 0: Normal.
  • 1: Having ST-T wave abnormality (T wave inversions and/or ST elevation or depression of > 0.05 mV).
  • 2: Showing probable or definite left ventricular hypertrophy by Estes' criteria.

Maximum heart rate achieved during the test.

Exercise induced angina.

  • 1: Yes
  • 0: No

ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest. Typical values range from 0 to 6.2.

The slope of the peak exercise ST segment:

  • 0: Upsloping (better heart health generally).
  • 1: Flat (neutral).
  • 2: Downsloping (unhealthy heart sign).

Number of major vessels (0-3) colored by flourosopy.

A blood disorder involving lower-than-normal amounts of an oxygen-carrying protein.

  • 0: Normal.
  • 1: Fixed defect (no blood flow in some part of the heart).
  • 2: Reversable defect (a blood flow is observed but it is not normal).

Diabetes Input Parameters

Number of times pregnant.

Plasma glucose concentration a 2 hours in an oral glucose tolerance test.

Diastolic blood pressure (mm Hg).

Triceps skin fold thickness (mm).

2-Hour serum insulin (mu U/ml).

Body mass index (weight in kg / (height in m)^2).

A function which scores likelihood of diabetes based on family history.

Age in years.
